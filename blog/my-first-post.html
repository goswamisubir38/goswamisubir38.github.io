AI & Storytelling

My First
Blog Post.

Subir Goswami March 2026·4 min read·Kolkata

Welcome to my journal. This is where I will explore ideas at the intersection of artificial intelligence, storytelling, and entrepreneurship — three things that have shaped every chapter of my career and, I believe, are reshaping every industry worth watching right now.

Why Write?

I have spent 14 years in boardrooms, on campaign floors, and in late-night strategy sessions where the best ideas were born not from data alone, but from someone who could tell a story clearly enough that a room full of sceptics became believers. Writing is that skill, practised in public.

"Technology is changing the way we create and share stories. The question is not whether AI will change your industry — it already has. The question is who tells that story first."

— Subir Goswami

What You Can Expect Here

Through this journal I want to explore how creativity and technology can work together — not in the abstract, but with specific observations from real campaigns, real ventures, and real mistakes. No recycled LinkedIn content. No generic AI listicles. Only things I have actually thought about.

A note on frequency

I write when I have something worth saying. That means posts may come weekly, or they may come after a long quiet spell followed by three in one week. Quality over cadence.

Topics I plan to explore

The intersection of AI and brand identity. What happens when a machine can generate a logo, a tagline, and a campaign brief in 30 seconds — and whether that is a threat or the most powerful tool a strategist has ever been handed. The craft of storytelling in business, specifically how the principles of literary fiction — tension, character, resolution — apply directly to marketing strategy. And the reality of building ventures in Kolkata, a city full of intelligence and ambition that the startup world consistently underestimates.

If any of that sounds worth your time — you are in the right place. Read on, push back, or write to me directly. Good thinking improves with friction.
